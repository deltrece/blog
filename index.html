Share the Road

June 1, 2023

In the State of Ohio, bicycles are considered road vehicles and may use the full lane when traveling on public streets. Using the full lane by the cyclist increases their visibility on the road. When you see a bicycle rider, it is similar to any other slower moving vehicle and give adequate distance when passing. Ohio State Law requires a minimum of 3 feet distance when passing a cyclist.
